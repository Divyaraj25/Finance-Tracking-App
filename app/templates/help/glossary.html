Financial Terms Glossary

Common financial terms used in Expense Tracker

{% for letter in 'ABCDEFGHIJKLMNOPQRSTUVWXYZ' %} {{ letter }} {% endfor %}
A
Account
A record of financial transactions for a specific purpose (checking, savings, credit card).
APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
The yearly interest rate charged on loans or credit cards.
Asset
An item of value owned by an individual or corporation (investments, property, vehicles).
Audit Trail
A chronological record of all system activities and changes.
B
Balance
The amount of money in an account at a specific time.
Budget
A spending plan that allocates income toward expenses and savings.
Bulk Operation
Performing the same action on multiple transactions at once.
C
Category
A classification for transactions to help with organization and reporting.
Credit
Money added to an account (income, deposits).
Credit Card
A revolving credit line that allows borrowing up to a limit.
Credit Limit
The maximum amount you can borrow on a credit card.
CSV
Comma-Separated Values - a file format for spreadsheet data.
D
Dashboard
The main overview page showing financial summaries and charts.
Debit
Money removed from an account (expenses, payments).
Debt
Money owed to others (credit cards, loans, mortgages).
Due Date
The date by which a payment must be made.
E
Expense
Money spent on goods or services.
Export
Downloading data in various formats (CSV, JSON, Excel).
F
Filter
A tool to narrow down transactions based on specific criteria.
Fixed Expense
Regular expenses that don't change month to month (rent, insurance).
G
Gross Income
Income before taxes and deductions.
I
Income
Money received from work, investments, or other sources.
Interest
The cost of borrowing money or earnings from savings.
Investment
Assets purchased with the expectation of future returns.
J
JSON
JavaScript Object Notation - a lightweight data format.
L
Liability
A financial obligation or debt.
Log
A record of system events and user actions.
N
Net Income
Income after taxes and deductions (take-home pay).
Net Worth
Total assets minus total liabilities.
P
Pagination
Dividing large datasets into smaller pages.
Principal
The original amount of a loan, excluding interest.
R
Reconciliation
The process of matching recorded transactions with bank statements.
REST API
A web service interface for programmatic access.
S
Statement Balance
The amount due on a credit card for the current billing cycle.
Savings
Income not spent on current expenses.
T
Tags
Additional labels for transactions beyond categories.
Transaction
A record of money moving in or out of an account.
Transfer
Moving money between your own accounts.
V
Variable Expense
Expenses that change month to month (groceries, entertainment).
