Managing Accounts

Core Concept: Accounts represent where your money lives. Every transaction must be linked to at least one account.

1. Introduction to Accounts

The Expense Tracker system supports multiple account types to accurately represent your complete financial picture. From traditional bank accounts to credit cards, assets, and liabilities - you can track everything in one place.

3. Creating Accounts

1
Navigate to Accounts Page

Click "Accounts" in the main navigation menu.

2
Click "Add Account" Button

The button is located at the top right corner of the Accounts page.

3
Fill Account Details
Required Information:
  • Account Name
  • Account Type
  • Initial Balance
  • Currency
Optional (Type-Specific):
  • Credit Limit (Credit Cards)
  • Due Date (Credit Cards)
  • Statement Balance (Credit Cards)
  • Interest Rate (Assets/Liabilities)
4
Set Initial Balance

Enter your current account balance. For credit cards and liabilities, use positive numbers for the debt amount.

Important: Credit card balances should be entered as POSITIVE numbers (e.g., $500 debt = 500, not -500).
5
Save Account

Click "Save Account" to create your account. It will appear immediately in your accounts list.

Credit Card Features
Credit Card Fields:

  • Statement Balance: Current statement amount
  • Available Credit: Automatically calculated
  • Usage Percentage: Balance √∑ Limit √ó 100
Credit Card Transactions:
  • Purchases: Record as EXPENSE transactions
  • Payments: Use CREDIT_CARD_PAYMENT type
  • Refunds: Record as negative expenses
  • Annual Fees: Record as expenses
Making Credit Card Payments
1
Select "Credit Card Payment" Transaction Type

This special transaction type correctly handles both accounts.

2
Choose Source Account

Select the bank account or cash account making the payment.

3
Choose Destination Account

Select the credit card account you're paying.

4
Enter Amount

Enter the payment amount. The system will:

  • Decrease your bank account balance
  • Decrease your credit card debt (make it less negative)
Credit Card Dashboard: View all your credit cards, balances, due dates, and usage percentages in the Credit Card Summary page.

5. Assets & Liabilities

üè¢ Assets

Assets are items of value that you own.

Examples:
  • Investment accounts (stocks, bonds)
  • Real estate properties
  • Vehicles
  • Cryptocurrency
  • Precious metals
  • Art and collectibles
Tracking Tips:
  • Update asset values periodically
  • Record purchases as ASSET_PURCHASE
  • Track appreciation/depreciation manually
üìâ Liabilities

Liabilities are debts and money you owe.

Examples:
  • Mortgages
  • Student loans
  • Personal loans
  • Car loans
  • Medical debt
  • Business loans
Tracking Tips:
  • Record payments as LIABILITY_PAYMENT
  • Track interest rates separately
  • Monitor remaining term/balance
Interest Rate Tracking:

For both assets and liabilities, you can store the interest rate. This helps with:

  • Understanding loan costs
  • Comparing investment returns
  • Financial planning
Note: Interest calculations are not automatic. Use the stored rate for your own calculations.

6. Editing Accounts

Note: Editing account details does NOT affect transaction history. To adjust balances, add a transaction.
1
Find the Account

Locate the account in the Accounts table.

2
Click Edit Button

Click the icon in the Actions column.

3
Modify Account Details

You can update:

  • Account name
  • Credit limit (credit cards)
  • Due date (credit cards)
  • Statement balance (credit cards)

4
Save Changes

Click "Update Account" to save your modifications.

Important: Account deletion behavior depends on whether the account has transaction history.
üìã Accounts WITH Transactions

  • Hidden from most views
  • Transactions remain intact

Reactivating Accounts: Deactivated accounts can be reactivated by editing the account and setting "Active" to true. This feature is coming in v1.1.0.

8. Understanding Account Balances

How Balances Work:

Account balances are automatically calculated based on all transactions. You never manually set a balance after initial creation.

Transaction Type Effect on Balance Example
Income + Amount Salary deposit increases balance
Expense - Amount Purchase decreases balance
Transfer (From) - Amount Money leaves account
Transfer (To) + Amount Money arrives in account
Credit Card Payment - Amount (bank) Payment reduces bank balance
Credit Card Payment + Amount (credit card) Payment reduces debt (makes balance less negative)
Balance Display:
  • Green = Positive balance (you have money)
  • Red = Negative balance (you owe money)

3
If They Match

Great! Your records are accurate. Consider marking transactions as reconciled.

4
