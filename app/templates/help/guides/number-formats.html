Number Formats

Did you know? Different countries use different symbols for decimal points and thousands separators. Expense Tracker allows you to choose the format that matches your region.

1. Introduction to Number Formats

Number formats determine how numbers, especially currency amounts, are displayed throughout the application. The main differences between formats are:

  • Decimal separator - The symbol used to separate the whole number from the fractional part
  • Thousands separator - The symbol used to group digits for large numbers
  • Placement of symbols - Whether separators are used and where they're placed

1,234.56

US/UK Format

Decimal: . | Thousands: ,

1.234,56

European Format

Decimal: , | Thousands: .

4. Changing Your Number Format

1
Go to Profile Settings

Click on your profile icon in the top right corner and select "Profile"

2
Navigate to General Settings

Click on the "General" tab in the settings section

3
Find Number Format Option

Look for the "Number Format" dropdown menu

4
Select Your Preferred Format

Choose from the available options:

  • 1,234.56 - US/UK format
  • 1.234,56 - European format
  • 1 234,56 - French format
  • 1'234.56 - Swiss format
5
Save Changes

Click "Save General Settings" to apply your new number format

The format will be applied immediately across all pages

6. Frequently Asked Questions

No, the number format only affects display. All numbers are stored internally in the same way, regardless of your display preference. This ensures data consistency and accurate calculations.

Currently, we support the four most common international formats. If you need a format not listed, please contact support and we'll consider adding it in a future update.

If numbers look incorrect, try refreshing the page. Some cached data may need to reload. If the issue persists, try clearing your browser cache or contact support.

CSV exports use standard formatting to ensure compatibility with spreadsheet software. The numbers in CSV files use the US/UK format (1,234.56) regardless of your display preference.

The number format is global and applies to all numbers regardless of currency. If you need to display different currencies with their local formats, consider using separate accounts with different settings.
